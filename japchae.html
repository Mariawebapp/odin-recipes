Japchae



Japchae known as Korean glass noodles, means mixture of vegetables. Japchae is a stir fry noodle dish (The noodles are made from sweet potato starch) mixed with vegetables (the orgin of the dish was created in a time where cow slaugther was prohibited, today meat can be added as preference).

It is one of Korea's most popular dishes as it is served at every occasion, it is known as the party dish.


Ingredients

  • 250g glass noodles
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 2 cups onions, julienned
  • 2/3 spring onions
  • 2/3 cups carrots, julienned

Sauce

  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 tbsp cooking oil, (of choice)
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1/8 tsp blackpepper

Directions

  1. Pre-soak noodles in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Add oil to pan. Stir fry carrots, add some onions, salt, and blackpepper. Top with spring onion.
    • Set aside in a small dish.
  3. Add oil to pan. Stir fry spinach (till spinach seem slightly withered), add some salt and blackpepper. Top with spring onion.
    • Set aside in a small dish.
  4. Make sauce. To a small bowl add minced garlic, sugar, soy sauce, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Bring a pot of water to boil and cook noodles for 10 minutes or until soft and chewy.
    • Strain while cooling the noodles until running water.
    • Set aside in a bowl.
    • Add sesame oil and mix well.
  6. Add stir fry vegetables to bowl of noodles. Add sesame seed oil and mix well.
  7. Add the sauce and mix well.
  8. Top with sesame seeds
  9. Serve.
